# A1 Audit — Reverse Lineage Validation Fetch Wave

## Scope Check
- `validation_fetch` now prefers `JungleBus transaction/get` for tx-level reverse-lineage repair.
- The fetch path is explicitly bounded by throttle, fetch budget, traversal depth, and visited-set semantics.
- Provider failure or provider throttling does not become an `illegal_root` verdict.

## What Landed
- JungleBus `getRawTx` client is throttled to `10 req/sec`.
- JungleBus provider diagnostics now advertise `validation_fetch` capability and the reverse-lineage throttle hint.
- Validation dependency resolution now walks parent ancestry backward until one of the allowed stop conditions is reached.
- Repair work items and ops status now store and expose stop reason, fetch count, visited count, and traversal depth.
- Default/source examples now position `validation_fetch` as JungleBus-first with Bitails/WhatsOnChain fallbacks.

## Residuals
- `trusted_root_reached` remains part of the public stop-reason contract, but the current transaction-level reverse-lineage worker primarily emits `valid_issue_reached`, `illegal_root_found`, `missing_dependency`, `budget_exceeded`, `provider_rate_limited`, and `provider_error`.
- The `10 req/sec` limiter is currently attached at the JungleBus raw-transaction client layer, not as a more granular per-subsystem scheduler quota.
- Frontend bundle-size warning remains out of scope for this wave.
